Spaced Repetition Systems

Often times when it comes to learning a new language, it can be difficult to learn and memorize a large number of vocabulary words, especially so for languages such as Chinese and Japanese which contain thousands of unique characters. One method that can aid one's journey is by using a concept known as spaced repetition systems, or SRS for short. To describe spaced repetition systems, we first must introduce the idea of spaced repetition, which is a learning technique in which one extends or shortens the interval between studying material - vocabulary for example - depending on if they correctly answer said material or not. Applying this concept to an SRS, this type of system will appropriately sort the vocabulary you've reviewed into categories based on whether you answered correctly and how much time has passed between review sessions. For example, an interval for studying a vocabulary word may start out at 10 minutes, and if you correctly memorize and recite the word's meaning after that interval is over, you can extend that time to 30 minutes. Alternatively if you got it wrong, you can try reducing the time to 5 minutes instead.
